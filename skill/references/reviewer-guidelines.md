## Universal Evaluation Dimensions

All major HCI/Vis conferences assess papers across core dimensions:

### 1. Contribution (Originality & Significance)

**What reviewers ask:**
- Is the research problem important and timely?
- Does the paper make a novel, non-trivial contribution?
- How does it differ from and advance prior work?
- Is the contribution clearly articulated?

**How to demonstrate strong contribution:**
- Clearly position your work relative to the state of the art
- Highlight what's new and why it matters
- Connect to broader HCI/Vis research themes
- Discuss potential impact and applications

### 2. Quality of Evidence (Rigor & Validity)

**What reviewers ask:**
- Is the methodology sound and appropriate for the claims?
- Are user studies well-designed and properly executed?
- Are technical evaluations rigorous and comprehensive?
- Are analyses appropriate and correctly applied?
- Are threats to validity acknowledged and addressed?

**How to ensure high quality evidence:**
- For user studies: justify sample size, tasks, measures, and analysis
- For technical work: include comprehensive evaluations and baselines
- Provide sufficient detail for replication
- Document limitations and threats to validity
- Use appropriate statistical methods when applicable

### 3. Clarity (Writing & Presentation)

**What reviewers ask:**
- Is the paper clearly written and well-organized?
- Can an expert understand and potentially reproduce the work?
- Are figures/tables clear, relevant, and properly explained?
- Is notation consistent and well-defined?
- Is the paper self-contained?

**How to ensure clarity:**
- Use consistent terminology throughout
- Define all terms and notation at first use
- Include reproducibility details (appendix acceptable)
- Have non-authors read before submission
- Ensure figures have self-contained captions

### 4. Reflection (Critical Thinking & Limitations)

**What reviewers ask:**
- Are limitations honestly and comprehensively discussed?
- Is there critical reflection on the work?
- Are future directions meaningful and grounded?
- Do limitations undermine the core claims?

**How to demonstrate strong reflection:**
- Include a dedicated Discussion or Limitations section
- Be honest about what your work does NOT do
- Explain why limitations don't invalidate the contribution
- Provide thoughtful future work directions

## CHI Reviewer Guidelines

### CHI Four Review Dimensions

CHI explicitly evaluates papers on four dimensions:

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Contribution** | Originality, significance, and positioning |
| **Quality of Evidence** | Rigor, validity, and appropriateness of methods |
| **Clarity** | Writing, organization, figures, reproducibility |
| **Reflection** | Limitations, critical thinking, future work |

### Scoring System (1-5 Scale)

| Score | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **5** | Strong Accept | Exceptional paper; top ~5% of submissions |
| **4** | Accept | Excellent paper; should be accepted |
| **3** | Borderline Accept | Good paper with some weaknesses |
| **2** | Borderline Reject | Some strengths but weaknesses outweigh |
| **1** | Reject | Serious flaws; should not be accepted |

### Reviewer Instructions

Reviewers are explicitly instructed to:

1. **Evaluate based on the four dimensions** - Contribution, Evidence, Clarity, Reflection
2. **Provide constructive feedback** - Specific, actionable suggestions
3. **Not penalize honest limitations** - Acknowledging weaknesses is encouraged
4. **Consider reproducibility** - Can the work be understood and potentially replicated?
5. **Look for ethical considerations** - Especially for human subjects research

### What Reviewers Should Avoid

- Superficial, uninformed reviews
- Demanding unreasonable additional work
- Penalizing authors for honest limitation acknowledgment
- Rejecting for missing citations to reviewer's own work
- Focusing on minor issues while ignoring major contributions

## UIST Reviewer Guidelines

### UIST Review Focus

UIST emphasizes technical contributions in user interface software and technology:

| Focus Area | What Reviewers Look For |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **Technical Depth** | Technical novelty and sophistication |
| **System Quality** | Implementation quality and robustness |
| **UI Design** | Quality of interface and interaction design |
| **Evaluation** | Rigorous technical and/or user evaluation |
| **Innovation** | Creative and novel approaches |

### Technical Evaluation Criteria

For systems papers:
- Technical soundness and correctness
- Performance and scalability
- Implementation complexity vs. benefit
- Novelty of technical approach

For interaction techniques:
- Effectiveness of the technique
- Implementation details
- Comparison to existing approaches
- User experience considerations

## IEEE VIS Reviewer Guidelines

### VIS Review Dimensions

IEEE VIS (TVCG) evaluates visualization papers on:

| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Contribution** | Novelty and significance to visualization |
| **Technical Quality** | Soundness of algorithms/techniques |
| **Evaluation** | Rigorous validation (user studies, benchmarks) |
| **Presentation** | Clarity of writing and figures |
| **Reproducibility** | Availability of code/data |

### Visualization-Specific Criteria

- Visual design quality and effectiveness
- Scalability to real-world data
- Insight generation capabilities
- Domain relevance (if applicable)
- Comparison to state-of-the-art visualization techniques

## What Makes Reviews Strong

### Following Constructive Feedback Principles

Good reviewers follow these principles:

1. **First understand** - Show you've read and understood the paper
2. **Highlight strengths** - Acknowledge what works well
3. **Identify weaknesses** - Specific concerns with reasoning
4. **Provide suggestions** - Concrete ideas for improvement
5. **Be respectful** - Maintain professional tone

### Review Structure Best Practices

**Strong Review Structure:**
```
Summary (1 paragraph):
- What the paper does
- Main contribution claimed

Strengths (3-5 bullets):
- Specific positive aspects
- Why these matter

Weaknesses (3-5 bullets):
- Specific concerns
- Why these matter
- Suggestions for addressing

Questions (2-4 items):
- Clarifications needed
- Things that would change assessment

Minor Issues (optional):
- Typos, unclear sentences
- Formatting issues

Overall Assessment:
- Clear recommendation with reasoning
- Scores on all dimensions (for CHI)
```

## Common Reviewer Concerns

### Contribution Concerns

| Concern | How to Pre-empt |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Incremental contribution" | Clearly articulate what's new vs prior work |
| "Similar to [paper X]" | Explicitly compare to X in Related Work |
| "Problem not important" | Justify why the problem matters |
| "Contribution unclear" | Highlight contributions in abstract, intro, bullets |

### Evidence Concerns

| Concern | How to Pre-empt |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Study underpowered" | Justify sample size, report power analysis |
| "No comparison to baselines" | Include state-of-the-art comparisons |
| "Threats to validity ignored" | Discuss threats and how you addressed them |
| "Statistics missing" | Include appropriate statistical tests |
| "No replication info" | Provide code/data/implementation details |

### Clarity Concerns

| Concern | How to Pre-empt |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Hard to follow" | Use clear structure, signposting, examples |
| "Figures unclear" | Self-contained captions, proper sizing, colorblind-safe |
| "Missing details" | Include reproducibility appendix |
| "Inconsistent terminology" | Review all terms, create glossary if needed |

### Reflection Concerns

| Concern | How to Pre-empt |
|---------|-----------------|
| "Limitations missing" | Include dedicated Discussion/Limitations section |
| "Future work vague" | Provide specific, grounded future directions |
| "No critical reflection" | Show you've thought deeply about your work |

## How to Address Reviewer Feedback

### Rebuttal Best Practices

**Do:**
- Thank reviewers for their time and feedback
- Address each concern specifically and thoughtfully
- Provide evidence (new data/explanations if possible)
- Be concise—reviewers are busy
- Acknowledge valid criticisms gracefully
- Stay professional and respectful

**Don't:**
- Be defensive or dismissive
- Make promises you can't keep
- Ignore difficult criticisms
- Write excessively long rebuttals
- Argue about subjective assessments
- Use adversarial tone

### Rebuttal Template for CHI/UIST

```markdown
We thank the reviewers for their thoughtful and constructive feedback.

## Reviewer 1

**R1-Q1: [Quoted concern]**
[Direct response with evidence or explanation]

**R1-Q2: [Quoted concern]**
[Direct response with evidence or explanation]

## Reviewer 2

...

## Summary of Changes

If accepted, we will revise the paper to address reviewer feedback:
1. [Specific change 1]
2. [Specific change 2]
3. [Specific change 3]

We will also address minor typos and formatting issues mentioned by reviewers.
```

### When to Accept Criticism

Some reviewer feedback should simply be accepted:
- Valid technical errors
- Missing important related work
- Unclear explanations or figures
- Missing experimental or study details

Acknowledge these gracefully: "The reviewer is correct that... We will revise to..."

### When to Push Back

You can respectfully disagree when:
- Reviewer misunderstood the paper
- Requested experiments are out of scope
- Criticism is factually incorrect
- Additional work would be unreasonable

Frame disagreements constructively: "We appreciate this perspective. However, [explanation]..."

## Pre-Submission Reviewer Simulation

Before submitting, ask yourself:

**Contribution:**
- [ ] Can I clearly state the contribution in 1-2 sentences?
- [ ] Have I clearly positioned this relative to prior work?
- [ ] Why should the HCI/Vis community care about this?

**Quality of Evidence:**
- [ ] Is my methodology appropriate for my claims?
- [ ] Have I justified all study/evaluation design decisions?
- [ ] Have I discussed threats to validity?
- [ ] Could an expert understand and potentially replicate this?

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Is the paper well-organized and easy to follow?
- [ ] Are all figures/tables clear and properly explained?
- [ ] Is terminology consistent and well-defined?

**Reflection:**
- [ ] Have I honestly discussed limitations?
- [ ] Have I provided thoughtful future work?
- [ ] Have I shown critical thinking about my work?
